## 🔑 Fundamental Principles of InfoSec

- **Confidentiality** → Only authorized access; use encryption & access controls
    
- **Integrity** → Data accuracy & completeness; use hashing & digital signatures
    
- **Availability** → Data accessible when needed; redundancy & disaster recovery
    
- **Non-repudiation** → Prevent denial of actions; digital signatures & audit logs
    
- **Authentication** → Verify identity; passwords, biometrics, MFA
    
- **Privacy** → Protect personal data; data minimization & consent management
    

## ⚙️ Key Processes in InfoSec

- **Risk Assessment** → Identify threats, evaluate impact, prioritize efforts
    
- **Security Planning** → Develop strategies, policies, allocate resources
    
- **Implementation of Controls** → Deploy technical solutions, enforce policies
    
- **Monitoring & Detection** → Use SIEM, IDS/IPS to spot anomalies
    
- **Incident Response** → Contain, eradicate, recover from threats
    
- **Disaster Recovery** → Restore systems/data, minimize downtime
    
- **Continuous Improvement** → Learn from incidents, update measures, audits
    

## 🎯 Purpose of InfoSec

- **Protect sensitive data** → Prevent breaches, safeguard personal/financial info
    
- **Ensure business continuity** → Keep systems running during incidents
    
- **Maintain compliance** → Follow laws & industry standards
    
- **Preserve reputation** → Avoid damage from breaches
    
- **Safeguard intellectual property** → Protect inventions & trade secrets
    
- **Enable secure digital transformation** → Adopt new tech safely
    

## 🛠️ Tools in InfoSec

- **Firewalls** → Control network traffic
    
- **IDS/IPS** → Detect & block suspicious activity
    
- **SIEM systems** → Collect & analyze security events
    
- **Vulnerability scanners** → Find weaknesses
    
- **Penetration testing tools** → Metasploit, Burp Suite
    
- **Encryption tools** → Protect confidentiality & integrity
    
- **Access control systems** → Manage permissions
    
- **Training platforms** → Educate users
